## Preamble
Thrive OS exists to compound knowledge, improve decision-making, and increase leverage over time. This Constitution establishes the purpose, principles, and authority under which the system is built, governed, and extended. It is foundational and deliberately concise: it states what must always be true, not how any particular part of the system works today.

## Article I — Purpose
Thrive OS shall exist to serve as a personal and business operating system built on structured knowledge, disciplined execution, and collaboration between its owner and AI systems. Every decision made under this Constitution must serve that purpose.

## Article II — Core Principles
1. Architecture must precede implementation.
2. Documentation must be treated as part of development, not an afterthought to it.
3. The Vault shall be the canonical source of truth for Thrive OS.
4. Decisions must be traceable to an approved document, not to memory or informal agreement.
5. AI must augment human judgment within its defined authority; it may not substitute for it.
6. Thrive OS shall remain modular and vendor-independent.
7. Governance must evolve only through controlled, deliberate amendments.

## Article III — Authority
Thrive OS shall be governed through a defined hierarchy of enduring roles, each operating within the boundaries this Constitution establishes:

- **Founder** — owns the vision of Thrive OS and retains final authority over its direction.
- **Chief Systems Architect** — maintains the architectural integrity of Thrive OS and provides architectural stewardship.
- **Lead Software Engineer** — implements approved architecture and specifications.
- **AI Roles** — operate only within authority explicitly delegated by the governance framework.

No role may act outside the authority defined here, and no artifact produced by any role may contradict this Constitution. The processes by which specific decisions, approvals, and artifacts are reviewed shall be defined in Governance documents, not in this Constitution.

## Article IV — Source of Truth
The Vault shall be the canonical source of truth for Thrive OS. Where documents or conversations conflict, authority shall be determined according to the following order of precedence:

1. This Constitution.
2. Governance Documents.
3. Approved Architecture Decision Records.
4. Approved Specifications.
5. Approved Implementations.
6. Conversations.

Conversations are never authoritative on their own. Where a conversation and the Vault disagree, the Vault must prevail, and the conflict must be resolved by updating the Vault, not by deferring to recollection.

## Article V — Documentation-First Development
Implementable work must be described in an approved specification before it is built. No permanent implementation shall begin without an approved specification. Documentation must be kept synchronized with what is actually implemented; work that changes system behavior without corresponding documentation must not be considered complete.

## Article VI — Governance
Decisions that affect the architecture, principles, or direction of Thrive OS must be made deliberately and recorded, not inferred from silence or assumed from prior conversation. The process by which such decisions are proposed, reviewed, and approved shall be defined in a dedicated governance document. *(See: Governance Process — placeholder, not yet written.)*

## Article VII — AI Collaboration
AI systems may implement approved specifications, build and improve software, and recommend enhancements, but may not unilaterally redesign the system's architecture or governance. AI collaboration must remain transparent: assumptions must be stated explicitly, and ambiguity must be raised for clarification rather than resolved silently.

The Thrive Vault shall remain the canonical coordination and knowledge layer for permanent work products, architectural decisions, and institutional knowledge. Detailed conventions for AI collaboration shall be defined in a dedicated document. *(See: AI Collaboration Conventions — placeholder, not yet written.)*

## Article VIII — Constitutional Amendments
This Constitution may be amended, but only deliberately. An amendment must be proposed explicitly, reviewed against the system's purpose and principles, and explicitly approved before it takes effect. No amendment may take effect through silence, inference, or informal agreement. Approved amendments shall supersede previous constitutional text and shall be versioned.

## Appendix — Definitions
- **Thrive OS** — the personal and business operating system this Constitution governs.
- **The Vault** — the structured, version-controlled body of documentation that serves as Thrive OS's source of truth.
- **Constitution** — this document; the supreme governing reference for Thrive OS, to which all other documents and decisions are subordinate.
- **Governance Document** — a document defining how decisions affecting Thrive OS are proposed, reviewed, and approved.
- **Architecture Decision Record (ADR)** — a document recording a significant, deliberate architectural decision and its rationale.
- **Specification** — a document defining what must be implemented, approved before implementation begins.
- **Implementation** — the realized system or artifact built to satisfy an approved Specification.
- **AI Roles** — capacities delegated to AI systems to implement, document, or recommend within authority granted by the roles defined in Article III.
